Studio Exercises

Practical exercises that connect your reading directly to your studio work and the city around you.

Phase 2 — Analysis
The X-Ray Analysis

Choose any canonical building. Draw 5 diagrams using the Precedents in Architecture method: 1. Natural light strategy 2. Movement/circulation 3. Structure 4. Unit/repetition 5. Massing/hierarchy. Do NOT copy the book's diagrams — derive them yourself from plans and sections.

Books: Precedents in Architecture · Analysing Architecture
Phase 3 — Phenomenology
The Sensory Map

Visit any building or urban space in Dhaka. Spend 30 minutes and document it using ONLY non-visual language — describe sounds, smells, temperature, tactile surfaces, the quality of air. No visual descriptions allowed. Then sketch what you experienced. Apply Zumthor's 9 atmospheres as your checklist.

Books: Atmospheres · Experiencing Architecture · The Eyes of the Skin
Phase 3 — Imagination
The Calvino City

Invent your own city in the style of Calvino's Invisible Cities. It must be named after a quality (not a place name), and it must be described in exactly 150 words. Then make a section drawing of that city. After drawing it, ask: what does this city actually want to be in architectural terms?

Books: Invisible Cities · The Poetics of Space
Phase 4 — Construction
The Detail Philosophy

Draw one construction detail at 1:5 scale for your current studio project. Then write 200 words explaining the philosophical position embedded in that detail. Why this material? Why this connection method? What does the detail say about your values? Reference at least one book from Phase 4.

Books: Building Construction Illustrated · Wood and Wood Joints · Architectural Detailing
Phase 6 — Theory
The Dhaka Pattern Survey

Open A Pattern Language. Select 10 patterns that Dhaka either fulfils beautifully or violates catastrophically. For each pattern, find a specific street, building, or neighbourhood in Dhaka and photograph or sketch it. Write one sentence of analysis per pattern. This is the beginning of a publishable research project.

Books: A Pattern Language · Death and Life of Great American Cities
Phase 6 — Critical Writing
The Two-Page Critique

Choose any recently completed building in Bangladesh. Write a two-page critical analysis using the vocabulary from your theory books. Paragraph 1: Spatial analysis (Unwin method). Paragraph 2: Phenomenological evaluation (Zumthor/Pallasmaa). Paragraph 3: Urban/contextual critique (Rossi/Jacobs). Paragraph 4: Your judgment — does it belong here?

Books: Analysing Architecture · Atmospheres · Architecture of the City
Phase 7 — Advanced
The Lefebvre Walk

Choose a 500m stretch of any Dhaka street. Walk it three times: first as a consumer (how space is perceived), then as a planner (how it was conceived), then as an inhabitant (how it is lived). Write a 300-word analysis using Lefebvre's three-part framework: perceived / conceived / lived space.

Books: The Production of Space · Space and Place (Yi-Fu Tuan)
Weekly Habit
The 15-Minute Sketch Rule

Every single day, sketch for 15 minutes without any goal. Not for a project. Not for an assignment. Just draw what you see, what you imagine, what you read. After 365 days you will have a complete visual archive of your intellectual year — more valuable than any portfolio piece.

Books: Freehand Sketching (Laseau) · Design Drawing (Ching)
